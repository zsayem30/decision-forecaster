## Assessment

### Strengths
1. Clean theoretical motivation: decomposing data informativeness into difficulty (aleatoric) and evidence (epistemic) via Beta-Binomial Bayesian model
2. Mutual information provides principled way to quantify expected uncertainty reduction
3. Broadband empirical validation across 0.6B-7B models, planning/math/general reasoning domains
4. Included strong ablative baselines (Expected-Difficulty, Inverse-Evidence) that isolate contributions

### My Observation: WMI condition number gap

The WMI acquisition score A(τ) = w(φ̄_τ) · I(R_1:K; Φ_τ) combines two terms operating at different scales. The mutual information term I(R;Φ_τ) is bounded above by H(Φ_τ), the entropy of a Beta(α,β) prior. With uniform prior (α=β=1), H ≈ 0.693 nats. The weighting function w(φ̄) has a maximum of 0.25 at φ̄=0.5 (when μ=0.5) and decays rapidly as η grows.

This creates a condition-number problem: the weighting function can zero out a datapoint's score regardless of how much epistemic uncertainty remains. Conversely, after many observations, I(R;Φ) → O(1/n) per Proposition 5.1, so fully-explored easy points get zero score even if they're at the optimal difficulty. This tradeoff is the paper's design — but absent a sensitivity analysis over the hyperparameters (η, μ, α, β), it's unclear whether the reported gains are robust to misspecification or whether per-task tuning is required.

The paper uses:
- Same η across all tasks (never explicitly stated)
- μ=0.5 implicitly (the variance term φ̄(1-φ̄) peaks at 0.5)
- Prior α, β set uniformly

A reader deploying InSight on a new task would need to know: if I set η=1 vs η=5, how much does performance change? If the target difficulty is 0.3 rather than 0.5 (more relevant for hard math problems), does WMI still help? These are not addressed.

### Verdict Lean
This is a solid paper — the method is well-motivated, the experiments are comprehensive, and the results are consistently positive. However, the missing sensitivity analysis on the 4 key hyperparameters and the artifact gap (no InSight-specific code) push this toward the low end of weak accept. I'd place it around 5.0-5.5.
