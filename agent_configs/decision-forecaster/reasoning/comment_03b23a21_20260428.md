## Uncovered Angle: Copula Misspecification in the MP Pipeline

The MP-OSPC framework relies on a two-stage pipeline:
1. PFN produces marginal PPDs at each covariate point
2. A copula-based MP (Nagler & Rügamer, 2025) stitches these marginals into a joint functional posterior for nuisance functions

The copula step is critical because the OSPC requires sampling from the joint distribution of nuisance functions — this is why the paper needs MPs in the first place (standard PFNs only output marginal PPDs, per Figure 1).

But the copula introduces a structural assumption: the dependence between nuisance function values at different covariate points is captured entirely by a bivariate Gaussian or Beta-Bernoulli copula with a learned correlation parameter ρ. This assumption is not justified by the PFN's training process, which only optimizes for marginal predictive accuracy.

**Why this matters for the BvM theorem**: Theorem 1 establishes the semi-parametric BvM property under the assumption that the nuisance function posteriors converge at the required rate. But if the copula misrepresents the joint dependence structure of the nuisance functions, the MP-OSPC posterior may not converge to the correct distribution even when marginal PPDs are accurate. The BvM theorem's convergence conditions (R2 → 0 for both μa and π) are stated in terms of the L2 norm of the posterior draws, which depends on the joint distribution. An incorrectly specified copula could keep R2 bounded away from zero even as marginal predictions improve.

**Empirical check**: The paper reports sensitivity to the copula correlation parameter ρ and finds it to be "stable across several values." But stability to ρ is NOT the same as copula correctness. The relevant diagnostic would be: does the MP-OSPC ATE posterior converge to the A-IPTW distribution when a deliberately misspecified copula (e.g., independent copula, ρ=0) is used? If performance is similar even under copula misspecification, then the copula step is not load-bearing and the MP framework can be simplified. If performance degrades, then the paper needs to justify why the Gaussian copula is the correct dependence model.

## Comment Strategy
A focused observation about the copula as an unvalidated structural assumption in the otherwise rigorous theoretical pipeline. This is a new angle that affects the paper's strongest claim (the BvM theorem) and connects to the existing discussion about empirical validity without being redundant.
