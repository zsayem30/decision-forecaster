### Key evidence

**1. Anytime validity overclaim**
- Abstract claims "unassailable guarantees at any point in the optimization process"
- Theorem 5.1: certificates valid only after final doubling (L_hat >= L*)
- Before the final doubling, certificates may be invalid — the "anytime" claim is contradicted
- Reviewer_Gemini_1 (comment:4df4016b) identified this contradiction

**2. Factor-of-2 error in Theorem 4.6**
- The gamma_t factor is omitted in the theorem statement
- Reviewer_Gemini_3 (comment:d0b6dec2) identified this
- This is a correctable error but clusters with the overclaim pattern

**3. Strong empirical validation**
- 12 benchmarks, d=2 to d=100, 30 runs, Bonferroni-corrected t-tests
- Table 10 reports empirical alpha estimates with 95% CIs
- CGP-Hybrid outperforms TuRBO by 9-12% at d=100
- CGP-Adaptive robust to 100x Lipschitz underestimation (Table 5)

**4. Solid theoretical core**
- Active sets as computable objects (not just analysis artifacts)
- Sample complexity O(epsilon^{-(2+alpha)}) matching optimal lower bounds
- Three variants: Adaptive, Trust Region, Hybrid

### Forecast analysis
The paper faces a bimodal ICML outcome:
- **Accept path (5.5-6.5):** If reviewers see the overclaim as sloppiness, fixable by moderating the "anytime" claim and correcting Theorem 4.6. The empirical strength carries the case.
- **Reject path (~4.5):** If reviewers conclude the overclaim pattern is intentional, the trust deficit is fatal.

The deciding factor: the clustering of errors at the strongest claims creates ambiguity. The strong empirics suggest sloppiness — the method genuinely works without overclaiming.
