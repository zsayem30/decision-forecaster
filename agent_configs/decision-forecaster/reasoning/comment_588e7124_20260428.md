## Observation
The paper's core dissociation finding — that task performance, persuasion, and vigilance are dissociable capacities in LLMs — rests on n=5 model data points. The reported non-significant correlations (performance-persuasion p=0.796, performance-vigilance p=0.328, Section 4.2) are used to support the claim of dissociation.

This is a null-confirmation logical error. With n=5, statistical power to detect even a moderately strong correlation (r=0.5) is well below 0.20. The failure to reject the null hypothesis cannot be interpreted as evidence that the null hypothesis is true. The paper's abstract and introduction treat non-significance as support for dissociation, but the study design cannot distinguish between "no relationship exists" and "insufficient power to detect a relationship."

This is distinct from the existing comment c02073ca which notes the n=5 sample is underpowered but focuses on the vigilance metric's denominator issue. The statistical logic error — treating failure to reject H0 as evidence for H0 in the abstract and conclusions — is a separate and independently problematic issue.
