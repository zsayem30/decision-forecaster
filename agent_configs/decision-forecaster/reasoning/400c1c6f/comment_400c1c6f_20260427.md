## Paper Summary
The paper identifies a previously unreported third phase of grokking called "anti-grokking" — a late-stage collapse of generalization where test accuracy plummets back to chance while training accuracy remains perfect. This is demonstrated in two canonical setups: a 3-layer MLP on MNIST subset and a transformer on modular addition, both trained far beyond standard durations (up to 10^7 steps). The authors use the WeightWatcher tool based on HTSR/SETOL theory to diagnose anti-grokking through two metrics: (1) Correlation Traps — anomalously large eigenvalues beyond the Marchenko-Pastur bulk in randomized weight matrices (primary signal), and (2) the HTSR power-law exponent α falling below 2 (secondary signal). The paper compares these diagnostics against prior grokking progress measures (ℓ₂ norm, Activation Sparsity, Absolute Weight Entropy, Local Circuit Complexity) and finds the prior measures fail to detect anti-grokking.

## Key Strengths
1. Genuinely novel phenomenon identification — anti-grokking is a distinct phase not previously characterized
2. Reproduced across two different architectures (MLP, transformer) and tasks
3. Theoretically-grounded diagnostics (SETOL theory predicts Correlation Traps cause generalization degradation)
4. Clear experimental design with WD=0 vs WD>0 ablation
5. Mechanistic insight through prototype memorization analysis (right singular vector visualization)
6. Compares favorably against existing grokking progress measures

## Key Weaknesses (Focus of Comment)

### 1. Predictive vs. Post-Hoc Detection Gap
The paper repeatedly claims WeightWatcher metrics "predict" and provide "early warning" for anti-grokking (lines 546, 693), but the evidence shows concurrent or slightly lagging detection. Figure 1 (training curves) shows test accuracy begins declining around 10^6 steps. Figure 4 (MLPtraps) shows Correlation Traps appearing at approximately the same point — the increase is coincident with, not prior to, the accuracy drop. The α metric in Figure 5 drops below 2 after anti-grokking is already underway. This is post-hoc detection, not prospective prediction in the strict sense. The framing should be "concurrent detection" rather than "early warning."

### 2. Single-Seed Limitation
The headline results use a single random seed (seed=0, stated in Table 1 of appendix). Table 1 in the main text reports ± values for α, suggesting some variance estimates exist, but the paper does not:
- Report multi-seed replication of the anti-grokking phenomenon itself
- Show whether the timing/magnitude of anti-grokking is stable across seeds
- Report whether Correlation Traps consistently appear at the same training step across seeds
- The WD>0 experiment (Appendix A) appears to also be single-seed

Given that grokking is known to exhibit substantial run-to-run variability (Power et al. 2022, Nanda et al. 2023), demonstrating anti-grokking with a single seed is insufficient to establish it as a robust phenomenon rather than a random training trajectory artifact.

### 3. Narrow Architectural Scope Mitigates Generalizability Claims
The experiments are limited to a 3-layer MLP (width 200) on 1000 MNIST samples and a small transformer on modular addition. The paper's final section (Figure 7) shows GPT-20B/120B layers with α<2 and implies anti-grokking risk in production LLMs, but:
- No anti-grokking is actually demonstrated in any large-scale model
- The LLM α<2 observation is purely correlational — we don't know if those layers correspond to degraded performance
- Many well-performing models have layers with α<2 (the paper acknowledges this in Limitations)
- The connection from α<2 to anti-grokking relies on the specific small-scale experimental setup, creating an extrapolation gap

### 4. Optimization Regime Artifact Risk
Training a 3-layer MLP for 10^7 steps with WD=0 and float64 precision represents an extreme optimization regime. The paper does not explore whether anti-grokking occurs under:
- Different optimizers (only AdamW tested)
- Different learning rates
- More standard training durations with higher-quality data
- Larger architectures where the phenomenon might manifest differently

The possibility that anti-grokking is an artifact of extreme overtraining rather than a general phenomenon affecting practical training should be explicitly discussed.
