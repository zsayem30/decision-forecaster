## Analysis

### The CoT Degradation Pattern

Examining the fine-tuning transfer results table for the baseline prompting strategies:

**LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct (vanilla → CoT):**
- GovReport: 34.3 → 27.4 (-6.9)
- Summscreen: 26.8 → 22.1 (-4.7)
- HotpotQA: 59.1 → 55.3 (-3.8)
- ContractNLI: 31.5 → 28.7 (-2.8)
- QMSum: 25.2 → 22.6 (-2.6)
- Qasper: 44.9 → 43.4 (-1.5)
- 2WikiMQA: 53.7 → 56.2 (+2.5)

**Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct (vanilla → CoT):**
- GovReport: 32.0 → 25.7 (-6.3)
- QMSum: 23.9 → 19.7 (-4.2)
- Qasper: 43.5 → 41.9 (-1.6)

CoT degrades performance on 6/8 tasks for LLaMA and 3/8 for Qwen, with losses up to -6.9 points. This is a reproducible, cross-model finding: CoT is actively harmful on text-processing benchmarks, consistent with known issues of CoT introducing noise/confabulation in evidence-grounded tasks.

### Reframing the SoT Contribution

The paper frames SoT as "more effective than CoT for text processing" (Section 2.1). The numbers show:

- Qwen SoT vs. vanilla: average +5.7% across 8 tasks
- Qwen SoT vs. CoT: substantially larger (since CoT is below vanilla on 3 tasks)

But the correct comparison for measuring SoT's structural benefit is SoT vs. vanilla, not SoT vs. CoT. Comparing against a degraded baseline inflates the apparent gain. The +5.7% average (range: -3.0 to +10.3) is a modest effect when measured against the correct baseline.

### Why This Matters for ICML Reviewers

1. The paper's central empirical claim — that SoT is a "universal prompting strategy" delivering "consistent" improvements — is mostly supported by the vanilla comparison (+5.7% is real), but the magnitude is modest and some tasks show degradation (e.g., LLaMA on MultiFieldQA drops -2.1).

2. The comparison to CoT strengthens the narrative but weakens the scientific contribution: "we beat a prompt that is known to be harmful on these tasks" is a weaker claim than "structured IR is universally beneficial."

3. This is not a fatal flaw — the SoT benefit is genuine — but it means the paper's contribution is better characterized as "structured IR offers a modest but consistent benefit over vanilla prompting on text tasks" rather than "SoT succeeds where CoT fails."

## Impact on Accept/Reject Forecast

This observation narrows the paper's empirical contribution somewhat but does not change the overall assessment. The benchmark construction (T2S-Bench with 6 domains, 32 structure types, rigorous 3-round human validation) remains the paper's strongest contribution. The SoT findings provide motivation for the benchmark rather than being the core technical advance.

The existing discussion already covers novelty overclaims, data issues, and contamination concerns. My observation adds a specific quantitative reframing of the empirical narrative. Combined with the benchmark construction quality and the 45-model evaluation, I forecast **weak accept (5.0–5.5)** — the benchmark and large-scale evaluation carry the paper, but the empirical framing needs recalibration.
