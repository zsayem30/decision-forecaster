## My Observation: Epistasis Claim vs. Approximation Bound

The paper's abstract states: "we prove that CoSiNE provides a first-order approximation to the intractable sequential point mutation process, capturing epistatic effects with an error bound that is quadratic in branch length."

This sentence conflates two distinct claims:

1. **Approximation error bound (true):** Proposition 4.1 shows that IF the learned rate matrices match the true instantaneous rates (Qθ(x))ℓ = Qx,y), THEN the factorized transition probability has O(t²) L1 error relative to the true sequential process.

2. **Capturing epistatic effects (unsupported by the theory):** The paper presents the O(t²) bound as evidence that CoSiNE "captures epistatic effects." But the bound controls the error between two processes (factorized vs. sequential) given the same rate matrices. It says nothing about whether the model actually learns the epistatic structure from training data.

The mechanism for capturing epistasis in CoSiNE is: a shared ESM-2 backbone conditions on the full parent sequence and produces site-specific rate matrices. This is the same mechanism used by any PLM with a per-site prediction head — the shared context encodes dependencies. The CTMC + matrix exponential formulation adds evolutionary time structure, but the mathematical guarantee (Proposition 4.1) bounds factorization error, not epistatic learning error.

Training on the factorized objective (Equation 2) means the learned rates are optimized for the factorized model, not for the true sequential process. The paper acknowledges this bias (lines 169-180: "its estimated rates will incur some bias due to the marginalization of unobserved intermediate states on long branches") but provides no bound on how far the learned rates diverge from truth. The O(t²) bound assumes correct rates — an assumption the training procedure violates.

## Verdict: Material but not fatal
This is not a fatal flaw — the empirical VEP results are genuine and the CTMC framework is creative. But it downgrades the theoretical contribution from "principled proof of epistasis capture" to "factorization error bound." Combined with the code artifact mismatch and K80 concerns, this paper lands in weak reject territory for ICML, absent a substantial revision.
