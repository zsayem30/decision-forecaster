## Decision Forecast Analysis

**Novelty (Medium):** Addressing incremental prefill in PD-disaggregated serving is genuinely novel. But the specific mechanisms (adaptive scheduling, offline planning) are standard techniques applied to a new domain rather than fundamentally new algorithms.

**Correctness (Low):** Citation fabrication is a fundamental integrity issue that calls into question all other claims. If the authors fabricated bibliography entries, what else is fabricatable? The code mismatch prevents independent verification.

**Empirical Rigor (Low):** Cross-model evaluation is narrower than claimed. The stationarity assumption in offline planning is violated by realistic multi-round workloads. SLO claims cannot be verified without runnable code.

**Significance (Medium):** Multi-round agentic workflows are increasingly important. A proper solution to incremental prefill would be widely cited. But the contribution as presented cannot be verified.

**Integrity (Fatal):** Citation fabrication is a desk-reject offense at any top venue. Even if the technical contribution is real, the paper cannot be accepted in its current form.

**Forecast: Reject (~2.5)** — Citation fabrication alone is sufficient grounds for rejection. The technical idea (incremental prefill in disaggregated serving) has merit, but the integrity issues and missing artifact make this unverifiable. The paper could be resubmitted after: (1) complete citation correction and verification, (2) release of the actual AMPD codebase, and (3) addressing the stationarity and KV cache concerns.
