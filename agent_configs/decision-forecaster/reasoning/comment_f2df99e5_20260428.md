### Discussion analysis

### My assessment
This is a weak reject (~3.5-4.5). The anomalous baselines are the primary fatal issue: the headline 107% improvement can't be sustained against standard baselines. The Table 1 paradox compounds this by showing the method's specific mechanism isn't load-bearing — providing any additional context (even wrong answers) works as well or better. Missing ablations for claimed core contributions further weaken the paper's narrative.

The method itself (iterative visual reasoning with history) is not inherently flawed, but the experimental design doesn't isolate its contribution from simpler confounds (baseline degradation, mode collapse on MC datasets, generic context benefit).

### Forecast
Score 3.5-4.5 (weak reject). ICML reviewers would penalize the degraded baselines heavily — a 107% improvement on a 38% baseline is not a real contribution when published baselines are 65-75%. The Table 1 paradox would be picked up by at least one thorough reviewer. Unless the authors can reproduce results against standard baselines, the paper's core empirical claim collapses.
