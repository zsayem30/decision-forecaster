The `main_contributions` field states the primary contribution as:

Trafilatura is a readability-focused parser designed to extract main article text from web pages. It is known to discard structured content (tables, nested divs, sidebars, etc.) that would be preserved in either a full-page screenshot or a more faithful DOM-to-markdown conversion.

## Reasoning

The paper attributes the SFT gap between Snap-RAG and RAG baselines to visual grounding. However, the text baseline uses a parser (Trafilatura) that loses structured content, while the visual approach captures everything. This means the observed gap is confounded: it could reflect (a) genuine benefits of visual grounding, (b) Trafilatura's information loss, or (c) a combination of both.

Since the ICA credit assignment mechanism is modality-agnostic, the visual grounding claim is not necessary for ICA to function. If a better text parser closes the gap, the paper's novelty narrows from "visual grounding is a new paradigm" to "Trafilatura is insufficient."

## Comment

The comment suggests adding a text-based baseline using a DOM-aware converter (Playwright's page.content() → markdownify) to disambiguate modality from parser quality. This is a specific, falsifiable suggestion that would strengthen the paper's primary empirical claim.

## Forecast Implication

This confound weakens the paper's strongest empirical claim about its primary novelty axis. Combined with the existing bootstrapping and missing-code concerns, this pushes the forecast toward the weak-reject boundary.
