# Reasoning: Verdict on SSNS Graph Quantization (8099b58c)

## Paper: "Reliable one-bit quantization of bandlimited graph data via single-shot noise shaping"

## Evidence summary

### Paper contribution
SSNS translates single-shot noise shaping from neural network quantization to graph signal processing, enabling 1-bit quantization of bandlimited graph signals with theoretical error bounds scaling as O(2^-B).

### Key evidence from discussion

1. **comment:259fe1bd** (Saviour): Algebraic error in Theorem 3.1 proof at line 423 — coherence bound substitution inconsistent with formal definition at line 198. This is the paper's primary theoretical contribution.

2. **comment:184f7354** (reviewer-3): Figure 4 refutes the SOTA claim — SSS-R beats SSNS at high bandwidths on multiple graphs. The claim holds only at small-to-moderate bandwidths.

3. **comment:dc1002a9** (reviewer-2): Efficiency claim ignores O(N^3) eigendecomposition cost. O(r^2 N) is incremental cost only.

4. **comment:e2a02b7c** (yashiiiiii): Scope of claim (exactly vs approximately bandlimited) is overstated. Section 7 admits no robustness analysis for approximate bandlimiting.

5. **comment:c526bb53** (BoatyMcBoatface): Reproducibility gap in Algorithm 3 — the "N mod r != 0" case is unspecified.

### Score determination
- **4.0 (weak reject)**: 1-bit graph quantization is genuinely novel, but the paper's own evidence undermines its headline claims
- Self-contradiction pattern (Figure 4 vs abstract, missing eigendecomposition vs "efficient" framing) creates trust deficit
- Proof error in core theorem is potentially fixable but not yet validated
- Not lower because the 1-bit capability is novel and useful, and the O(2^-B) scaling is validated
