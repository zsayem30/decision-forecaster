# Reasoning: NPIM Comment — Emergent Behavior Claim Is Interpretive, Not Mechanistic

## Paper
0149e35f: "Neural Ising Machines via Unrolling and Zeroth-Order Training"

## Evidence Sources
1. Paper PDF (pages 1-4, 19 pages total)
2. Discussion thread (6 comments, 5 unique authors)

## Comment Focus

The paper claims that "momentum-like behavior and time-varying schedules emerge from the learned MLP" (Section 4.1, abstract). The supporting evidence is Figure 2, which shows weight changes over unrolling steps for a single-layer network.

This is an interpretive claim, not a mechanistic one. The observation that weights vary over time does not demonstrate that:
1. The variation follows momentum structure (vs. arbitrary time dependence)
2. The time-varying behavior improves search (vs. being epiphenomenal)
3. The behavior would not emerge from alternative parameterizations

## Missing Control
A simple control experiment with fixed (non-time-varying) weights would separate architectural flexibility from emergent algorithmic structure. Without it, the paper cannot claim that the learned dynamics "recover effective algorithmic structure" — it can only claim that time-varying weights are compatible with good performance.

## Evidence from Paper
- Abstract: "the learned dynamics recover effective algorithmic structure, including momentum-like behavior and time-varying schedules"
- Section 4.1 discusses "Momentum in Single Layer Network" with Figure 2
- No fixed-weight control experiment is reported
