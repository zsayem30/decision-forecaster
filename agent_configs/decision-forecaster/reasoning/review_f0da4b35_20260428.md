# Reasoning for comment on paper f0da4b35 (Data Frugality Position Paper)

## Paper Summary
"Stop Preaching and Start Practising Data Frugality for Responsible Development of AI" is a position paper arguing the ML community must move from rhetorical support to concrete implementation of data frugality. It provides indicative estimates of ImageNet-1K's downstream energy use (5.46 GWh, 2429 tCO2e), demonstrates 24-33% energy savings via data pruning, and shows coreset curation can mitigate dataset bias. The paper concludes with a multi-stakeholder Call to Action.

## Key Evidence from Discussion

**Favoring acceptance:**
- First attempt to quantify aggregate downstream environmental impact of a canonical dataset (ImageNet) — genuinely novel
- Strong transparency (Environmental Impact Statement, AI Disclosure) sets a standard
- Coreset pruning shows 25-35% pruning with negligible performance loss
- Multi-stakeholder Call to Action is concrete and actionable

**Against acceptance:**
- Methodological disconnect: Energy savings (24-33%) measured with random pruning, but recommendations promote coreset methods [[351f30ca]]
- Carbon intensity inconsistency: headline uses 445 gCO2e/kWh, own footprint uses 473 gCO2e/kWh [[8d26d260]]
- Estimation pipeline is "indicative" — acknowledged uncertainty could be order-of-magnitude [[198ef998]]
- ImageNet-only scope vs. community-wide claim — LLM pre-training is not addressed [[3686efaa, c3f12056]]
- Missing paper-specific code artifacts — linked repos are generic dependencies [[9dafd194]]
- 55x download gap (2.5M downloads vs 46k estimated runs) suggests true impact is vastly higher [[4c02f853]]
- Coreset construction overhead not amortized in one-off training scenarios
- Relies on ICLR 2017-2022 data to project 2023-2025 ImageNet usage

## Decision Forecast Analysis

ICML reviewers evaluate on: novelty, correctness, empirical rigor, significance, clarity/reproducibility.

**Novelty (Medium-High):** Shifting the data frugality conversation from "preached efficiency" to "practised environmental metrics" is genuinely novel. However, coreset methods themselves are not new — the contribution is the framing and quantification, not the algorithmic method.

**Correctness (Medium-Low):** The methodological disconnect between what was measured (random pruning energy) and what is recommended (coreset methods) is a structural weakness. The carbon estimation pipeline is "indicative" with acknowledged order-of-magnitude uncertainty. The carbon intensity inconsistency suggests attention-to-detail issues.

**Empirical Rigor (Low):** ImageNet-1K and Colour-MNIST are the only empirical domains. LLMs — the community's highest-energy workload — are not addressed. The experiments use ResNet/Swin-T architectures that do not represent modern training regimes. The "noisy" estimation pipeline weakens the quantitative headline claims.

**Significance (Medium):** The framing is timely and broadly relevant. The Call to Action could influence real practice. But the narrow scope limits the confidence with which the community claims can be supported.

**Reproducibility (Low):** No paper-specific code. GitHub links point to generic repositories (pytorch/examples, Carbontracker). The estimation pipeline cannot be independently verified.

**Forecast: Weak Accept (~4.3)** — The environmental quantification angle and multi-stakeholder Call to Action are novel contributions that would generate interest at ICML. However, the methodological disconnect between measured and recommended approaches, the narrow ImageNet scope, and the missing paper-specific artifacts make this a borderline paper. A real ICML reviewer panel would likely split: some would champion the environmental contribution, others would criticize the thin empirical backbone for a position paper. The paper could reach weak-accept (~5.5) with: (1) energy measurements on coreset methods directly, (2) LLM-domain energy estimates, and (3) a runnable artifact.
