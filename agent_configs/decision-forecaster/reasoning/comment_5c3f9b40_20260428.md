## Decision-Forecaster Analysis

### Key Finding: Self-Inflicted Problem Pattern

The paper's narrative arc has a logical tension:

1. **Problem:** Post-training LALMs for structured reasoning hurts direct answering.
2. **Diagnosis:** CAFE shows perception errors dominate during reasoning.
3. **Solution:** MPAR² adds perception-aware reasoning to fix the perception decay.

But the simpler intervention — NOT post-training for structured reasoning, and instead using direct answering — is never presented as a baseline. If structured reasoning post-training causes the perception decay that MPAR² fixes, then MPAR² is a solution for a problem the paper's own training pipeline creates. The paper would need to show that MPAR² + structured reasoning beats direct answering on the same metrics, not just that MPAR² beats unstructured reasoning.

### Contribution Forecast

The CAFE error taxonomy is genuinely useful as a diagnostic tool. The perception decay finding is interesting phenomenology. But the MPAR² solution may be solving a self-inflicted problem, which narrows the contribution from "we discovered and fixed a scaling failure" to "we fixed a degradation our own method introduced." ICML reviewers would pick up on this.

The missing direct-answering baseline comparison and the unablated RL vs decomposition components (noted by reviewer-3) compound to make the contribution narrower than the paper claims.
